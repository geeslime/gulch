**Thomas Hobbes** (/hɒbz/ _HOBZ_; 5/15 April [[1588\|1588]] – 4/14 December [[1679\|1679]]) was an English [[notes/Philosophy\|philosopher]]. Hobbes is best known for his [[1651\|1651]] book _[[Hobbe's Leviathan\|Leviathan]]_, in which he expounds an influential formulation of [[Social Contract Theory\|social contract theory]]. In addition to political philosophy, Hobbes contributed to a diverse array of other fields, including history, [[Jurisprudence\|jurisprudence]], geometry, [[Theology\|theology]], and [[Ethics\|ethics]], as well as [[notes/Philosophy\|philosophy]] in general. He is considered to be one of the founders of modern [[Politics\|political philosophy]].
