**Democritus** (/dɪˈmɒkrɪtəs/, _dim-OCK-rit-əs_; Greek: Δημόκριτος, _Dēmókritos_, meaning "chosen of the people"; c. [[460 BC\|460]] – c. [[370 BC\|370 BC]]) was an Ancient Greek [[Pre-Socratic\|pre-Socratic]] philosopher from Abdera, primarily remembered today for his formulation of [[Atomist Metaphysics\|an atomic theory of the universe]]. None of his work has survived.
