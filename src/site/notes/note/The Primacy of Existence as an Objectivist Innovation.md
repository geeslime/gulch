>There were once Western philosophers who upheld [[note/The Primacy of Existence vs The Primacy of Consciousness\|the primacy of existence]]; notably, such ancient Greek giants as [[Parmenides\|Parmenides]] and [[people/Aristotle\|Aristotle]]. But even they were not consistent in this regard. (Aristotle, for example, describes his [[Aristotle's Unmoved Mover\|Prime Mover]] as [[note/A Consciousness Conscious Only of Itself\|a consciousness conscious only of itself]], which serves as the [[note/The Law of Causality\|cause]] of the world's motion.) There has never yet been a thinker who states the principle explicitly, then applies it methodically in every branch of philosophy, with no concession to any version of its antithesis. This is precisely what [[people/Ayn Rand\|Ayn Rand]] does. [[note/Objectivism\|Her philosophy]] is the primacy of existence come to full, systematic expression in Western thought for the first time.[^1]

[^1]: [[notes/OPAR\|notes/OPAR]]OPAR]], p. 23
