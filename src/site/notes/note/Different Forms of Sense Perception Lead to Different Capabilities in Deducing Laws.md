Man is capable of directly [[note/Percept\|perceiving]] and thus [[note/The Role of the Senses\|validating the existence of]] a great deal of physical phenomena---he knows that cars exist, and that they [[note/The Law of Identity\|have certain properties]] which [[note/The Law of Causality\|cause]] them to have various colours and whatnot. But, it takes a great deal more [[note/Concept\|conceptual]] [[Deduction\|deduction]] for him to discover the properties of various [[Sub-Atomic\|sub-atomic]] phenomena.[^1] On the other hand, if there were sub-atomic creatures with conceptual [[note/Consciousness\|consciousnesses]], they could directly [[Induction\|induce]] these [[note/Fact\|facts]] about the Quantum realm, and would have a much harder time deducing facts about macro-scopic objects---the upshot being that [[note/Different Forms of Sense Perception does not Invalidate Sense Perception\|having different forms of sense perception does not invalidate sense perception]].

[^1]: I do not use this in [[Phenomenal\|the Kantian sense]].
