The reason that [[note/Objectivism\|Objectivism]] rejects agnosticism [[note/The Arbitrary as Neither True nor False\|should be clear]]. This term applies not only to [[note/God as a Rejection of the Basic Axioms\|the question of God]], but also to many other issues, such as:

- [[ESP\|ESP]]
- [[note/Past Lives as an Example of The Arbitrary\|Reincarnation]]
- [[note/Mysticism\|Demonic possession]]
- [[Astrology\|Astrology]]
- The Arab claim of an international Zionist conspiracy
- [[note/The Error in Regarding the Man-Made as Immutable\|The Marxist claim that the state will wither away]]

In regard to all such issues and claims, the agnostic says: "We can't prove that the claim is [[note/Truth\|true]]. But we can't prove that it is [[note/Falsehood\|false]], either. So the only proper conclusion is: we don't know; no one knows; perhaps no one ever can know."

Agnosticism is not simply [[The Ad Ignoratiom Fallacy\|the pleading of ignorance]]. It enshrines ignorance as a philosophical viewpoint, demanding such a stance in response to claims devoid of [[note/Evidence\|Evidence]]. This viewpoint presents itself as fair, balanced, and impartial but is riddled with [[note/Logical Fallacies\|fallacies]] and prejudices. Agnostics treat [[note/Arbitrary\|arbitrary]] claims as matters deserving consideration, discussion, and evaluation. [[note/The Error of Conflating the Potential with the Actual\| They allow that it is "possible" these claims might be "true,"]] thereby [[note/Evasion\|misapplying cognitive descriptions]] to verbiage opposed to cognition. [[note/Onus of Proof Principle\|They demand proof of a negative]], asserting it is up to others to demonstrate that there are no demons or [[note/Past Lives as an Example of The Arbitrary\|that one's current life is not the result of a past life as an ancient Egyptian pharaoh.]]

The passion for the arbitrary does not stem from a concern for [[logic\|logic]] but from [[note/The Secondary Choice as Being Motivated\|emotions given precedence over reason]]. For some agnostics, this feeling is cowardice—*"a fear of antagonizing people by taking a stand on contentious issues."* For others, it is a more convoluted emotion akin to glee—*"the malicious pleasure of subverting all ideas and baiting those with the integrity to hold convictions."* This is the glee of the destroyer, the [[Mystics of Muscle\|mind-hater]], the [[Nihilism\|nihilist]].

In conclusion, [[logic\|logic]] is man's method of[[knowledge\|knowledge]] and cannot be defaulted on without severe consequences, as emotionalists of all kinds attempt to do. [[note/The Psychological Effects of Regarding the Metaphysically Given as Mutable\|Such a default exacts a fearsome toll]]—[[note/Epistemology\|epistemologically]], the worst there is: it removes the mental process from the realm of cognition. One cannot get something for nothing—not in material wealth nor in the field of knowledge. Truth, like knowledge, cannot be reached by accident but only through a process of [[Reason\|reason]].[^1]

[^1]: Paraphrased from [[notes/OPAR\|notes/OPAR]], p. 169-171
