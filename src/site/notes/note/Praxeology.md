Praxeology is the general theoretical [[note/Science\|science]] of [[note/Human Action\|human action]]. [[G2/ppp/The Special Nature of Theoretical Knowledge in the Field of Man\|It is a formal, universally valid, science]]—built on the insights of [[Mises\|Ludwig von Mises]], namely that [[G2/ppp/Methodological Individualism\|man is an individual]] [[note/The Validation of Free Will\|with volition]] and that this power to choose [[note/Human Action is Undeniable\|must be taken as a fundamental starting point]]. Praxeology refers to the set of sciences that derive by [[note/Inference\|logical inference]] exclusively from the axiom of human action. Once it is demonstrated that human action is a necessary [[G2/ppp/Capacities and Capabilities\|capacity]] of human beings, praxeology, and its daughter sciences, such as [[note/Economics\|economic theory]] and [[note/Law\|legal theory]], spins out by [[G2/ppp/Verbal Deduction vs. Mathematical Logic\|verbal deduction]] the the logical implications of this [[note/Concept\|concept]]. Praxeological laws are universal doctrines whose applicability is *independent* of any [[Particular\|particular]] circumstances. Praxeology is a unifying framework that unites all types of human actions, and interactions. 

Human beings possess a [[note/The Law of Identity\|stable nature]] with certain definite, [[note/The Role of Definition for a Conceptual Being\|definable]], and delimitable [[note/Characteristics vs. Properties (BPC)\|characteristics]]. [[note/Consciousness\|Consciousness]] and [[note/Free Will\|free will]] are essential attributes of [[note/Man\|man’s nature]]; [[G1/Man's Capacity to Reason\|Man's Capacity to Reason]] is man’s absolute guide. [[note/The Locus of Human Free Will\|One’s actions are caused by one's own volition which is a capacity of human nature]]. A human being can *initiate* and make choices about what he will do. Human action involves purposeful, intentional, and [[note/Normativity\|normative]] behavior. [[note/Mental Focus\|Mental focus]] is primary, and includes the direct willing of the person. Purposeful behavior thus takes place after a [[Value-Judegment\|judgment]] or [[note/The Objectivist Theory of Concepts\|conceptualization]] has been made. 

[[G2/ppp/Errors Resulting from the Failure to Recognize the Formal Nature of the Theoretical Study of Man\|Praxeology as a methodology focuses on the formal]] implications of the [[note/Fact\|fact]] that [[Means and Ends\|men employ means to attain various chosen ends]], differentiating it from [[psychology\|psychology]] and [[G3/boob/Autistic vs. Inter-Personal Ethics\|autistic ethics]], which deal with the *content* of human ends. Thus, praxeology is a [[G2/ppp/Value-Free Science\|value-free science]] of means, rather than of ends, that *describes* but does *not prescribe*. It is a value-free tool for objective and critical appraisal. Praxeological science differentiates between the objective, *universally* [[note/Validation\|valid]] conclusions of praxeology and the *personal* [[Value-Judegment\|value judgments]] of the praxeologist. The credibility of economic science depends upon an impartial and dispassionate concern for [[note/Truth\|truth]]. Value-freedom is a methodological device designed to separate and isolate an economist’s scientific work from the personal preferences of the given economic researcher. His goal is to maintain neutrality and objectivity with respect to the [[note/An Anthropogenic Theory of Value\|personally estimated values of others]].  
  
[[G2/ppp/Economics Nature and Its Uses\|The province of praxeological economics]] is the [[Logic\|logical]] analysis of the success or failure of selected means to attain ends. Means only have value because, and to the degree that, their ends are valued. [[G2/pilll/Thymology\|The reasons why an individual values what he values]] and [[Ethics\|the determination of whether or not his choices and actions are morally good or bad]] are certainly significant concerns but they are *not* the realm of the praxeological economist. The *content* of moral or ultimate ends is not the domain of the *economist qua economist*. [[G1/Knowledge\|Knowledge]] gained from praxeological economics is both value-free (i.e., value neutral) and value-relevant. Value-free knowledge supplied by economic science is value-relevant *when* it supplies information for rational discussions, deliberations, and determinations of the *morally* good. 

[[The causal-genetic method of praxeology\|The causal-genetic method of praxeology]] developed via a process of [[new 2/The Validity of Introspection\|introspection]] as part of a logical process based on [[Deduction\|deductive reasoning]] from [[Induction\|inductively-derived concepts]].   

 > [[note/Consciousness Has an Object and a Subject\|What is known (i.e., the object) is distinct from, and independent of, the knower (i.e., the subject)]]. Knowledge is gained via various processes of [[note/Integration\|integration]] and [[note/Differentiation\|differentiation]] from [[note/Sense Perception\|perceptual data]]. For example, a person [[note/The Axiom of Consciousness\|apprehends that he has a conscious mind]] by distinguishing between external objects and events and the workings of his mind. Self-awareness is thus attained when a person reflects upon what he has [[note/Sense Perception\|observed]]. Reality is what there is to be perceived. [[note/The Primacy of Existence vs The Primacy of Consciousness\|Reality exists independently of a man’s consciousness]]. [[note/Fact\|It exists apart from the knower]]. It follows that [[note/Empiricism\|empirical]] knowledge is acquired through observational experience of external reality. People can observe goal-directed actions from the outside. An individual attains an understanding of [[note/The Law of Causality\|causality]] and other categories of action by observing the actions of others to reach goals. He also learns about causality by means of his own acting and his observation of the outcomes. *Action is thus a man’s conscious adjustment to the state of the world*. 
> 
> Introspection is a reasonably reliable but *ancillary* source of evidence and knowledge with respect to what it means to be a rational, purposeful, volitional, and acting human being. Each person knows universally from introspection that he chooses. In other words, observation is introspective in the case of free will. Universal inner or reflective experience is an important adjunct to external, empirical, physical experience. [^1]

The [[Qualitative\|qualitative]] nature of praxeological analysis is also noteworthy. Unlike the [[Quantitative\|quantitative]] focus of many scientific disciplines, this strict methodological approach is essential for studying human behavior, which is inherently complex and variable. *The goal is to uncover the [[note/Causal Connections (BPC)\|causal]] laws governing human action*, and by extension, economic phenomena. Exact economic laws are established through a precise understanding of the way typical economizing individuals react to given situations. Once it is acknowledged that [[note/Human Action\|purposeful behavior]] is the typical behavior of economizing individuals, economists can logically derive theorems that will make up what [[Carl Menger\|Menger]] terms an exact and absolutely true theory.[^2] [[G2/ppp/The Special Nature of Theoretical Knowledge in the Field of Man\|Theoretical research seeks to identify the simplest and strictly typical elements of everything real]]. The individual and his behavior are the most basic elements by means of which the praxeologist explains economic phenomena and derives universal laws. Praxeology is built on the basis of the idea that there are, in the realm of economic phenomena, indispensable structures to every action that are manifested in every economy. Economic universals involve economizing action on the part of individuals. These universals of economic reality are discovered through theoretical efforts and are not [[note/Arbitrary\|arbitrary]] creations of the economist.   

The method of praxeology involves constructing *imaginary* constructs, or *hypothetical* scenarios, *to isolate and analyze specific aspects of human action* with [[note/Inference\|inferential]] chains of [[note/Proposition\|propositions]]. These constructs are not intended to represent reality directly but to *help deduce the logical consequences of certain actions*. For example, Rothbard's use of [[The Evenly Rotating Economy\|the evenly rotating economy]] (ERE) [[note/Abstraction\|abstracts]] from [[Change and Uncertainty\|change and uncertainty]] to analyze [[Interest Income\|interest income]] and [[G3/The Function of the Capitalist\|the capitalist function]]. The evenly rotating economy is a *hypothetical* construct where all economic activities are in a constant state of [[Equilibrium\|equilibrium]]. In this scenario, there is *no* change; every day is *exactly the same* as the previous one. This means that *all variables* such as [[Price\|prices]], [[Production (Economics)\|production]], and [[Consumption and Leisure\|consumption]] are stable and predictable. These constructs serve as tools to better understand the complex [[note/Causal Connections (BPC)\|causal relationships]] in the real economy. These economic universals exist only as instantiated in specific actions and institutuions. [[G2/ppp/Errors Resulting from the Failure to Recognize the Formal Nature of the Theoretical Study of Man\|The goal of theoretical research is to discover the simplest elements of all things real which must be apprehended as strictly typical merely because they are the simplest]]. Of course, it is not an easy matter to discover those structures and to construct workable theories about them. There may be huge difficulties in gaining knowledge of essential structures and in converting such knowledge into the organized system of a strict theory.
