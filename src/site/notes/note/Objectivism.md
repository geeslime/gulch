<strong><em>Objectivism</em></strong> is the philosophy developed by [[people/Ayn Rand\|Ayn Rand]] and derived from [[people/Aristotle\|Aristotle]], which is based on the fact that [[note/Existence Exists\|reality exists]] as an [[note/The Meaning of Objectivity\|objective]] absolute, that man's mind--his [[Reason\|reason]]--is his sole means of gaining knowledge of it, and that man needs [[Ethics\|a rational morality]]. The <em>essential</em> characteristic of Objectivism as against every other philosophy is [[note/The Primacy of Existence as an Objectivist Innovation\|the explicit identification and application of the primacy of existence]]. <em>Objectivity</em> means such an application---one is being <em>objective</em> in their activities if they are either implicitly or explicitly operating on the premise that [[note/The Primacy of Existence vs The Primacy of Consciousness\|existence has metaphysical primacy over consciousness]].
