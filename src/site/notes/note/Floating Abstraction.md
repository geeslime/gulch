A "floating abstraction" is any concept without a [[Abstract vs. Concrete\|concrete]] referent. These are concepts that are [[note/Bundled Philosophy\|borrowed from man to man second hand]] without [[note/The Objectivist Theory of Concepts\|the requisite foundation to relate it to existence]].

> A floating abstraction is not an integration of [[note/Fact\|factual]] data; it is a memorized linguistic custom representing in the person's mind a hash made of random concretes, habits, and feelings that blend imperceptibly into other hashes which are the content of other, similarly floating abstractions. The "concepts" of such a mind are not cognitive devices. They are parrotlike imitations of language backed in essence by patches of fog.[^1]

[^1]: [[notes/OPAR\|notes/OPAR]]OPAR]], 96
