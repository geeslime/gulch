**Normativity** refers to the set of [[Principles\|principles]] and [[Fundamentality\|standards]] derived from [[note/Entity and Property (BPC)\|the nature of reality]] in [[note/Relationship (BPC)\|relation]] to [[note/Man\|the nature of man]], *guiding* [[note/Human Action\|human actions]] and [[Value-Judegment\|evaluations]] in the [[Context\|context]] of promoting one's [[Value\|values]]. These principles [[note/Validation\|establish]] what is good or bad, right or wrong, etc. **Normative** relates to an [[Life as the Standard of Value\|evaluative standard]], providing criteria for *judging* the value of [[note/Human Action\|actions]], [[Thought\|thoughts]], and [[Emotions\|emotions]](and their [[note/Characteristics vs. Properties (BPC)\|characteristics]] e.g. like [[Means and Ends\|some particular means and the various ends one can chose to attain with it]]) based on their [[The Law of Non Contradiction\|alignment]] with these principles.

[Normative abstractions](http://aynrandlexicon.com/lexicon/normative_abstractions.html#:~:text=Normative%20abstractions%20are%20formed%20by%20the%20criterion%20of%3A%20what%20is%20good%3F&text=Consider%20the%20long%20conceptual%20chain,electronic%20computer%20could%20approach%20it.) in [[note/Objectivism\|Objectivism]] are [[note/The Objectivist Theory of Concepts\|formed]] by *evaluating* [[facts\|facts]] to *prescribe* a choice of values and a course of action. While cognitive abstractions deal with identifying facts (what is), normative abstractions deal with what *ought* to be, *guiding* human actions by rational, objective standards derived from reality. [[Logic\|Logic]] is a normative [[note/Science\|field]] because it [[note/The Validation of Free Will\|presupposes volition]] and *guides* thinking towards [[note/Truth\|true]] conclusions. It provides the necessary *norms* and *standards* to acquire [[note/The Epistemic Sense of Objectivity\|objective knowledge of reality]].
