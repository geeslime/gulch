To refute means: "to demonstrate a clash with something already [[Knowledge\|known]], even if only with [[The Law of Non Contradiction\|the law of non contradiction]]."

For instance, consider someone claiming there is a "round square" behind Venus. If you argue that [[Invalid Concept\|such a concept contradicts the very laws of logic]], and the claimant responds that [[note/The Kantian Attack on Cognition\|you need a higher perspective to understand the round square]], you cannot refute [[note/Arbitrary\|this claim]]. It defies logical principles and [[note/The Arbitrary as Neither True nor False\|cannot be processed cognitively]].
