>The idealists--figures such as [[people/Plato\|Plato]], [[Plotinus\|Plotinus]], [[Augustine\|Augustine]], [[Hegel\|Hegel]]--regard reality as a [[note/Spiritual vs Material\|spiritual]] dimension transcending and controlling the world of nature, which latter is regarded as deficient, ephemeral, [[note/The Timaeus on the Imperfection of Reality\|imperfect]]---in any event, as only partly real. Since "spiritual," in fact, has no meaning other than "[[Subjective\|pertaining to consciousness]]," the content of true reality in this view is invariably some function or form of consciousness (e.g., [[Plato's World of Forms\|Plato's abstractions]], [[Augustinian God\|Augustine's God]], [[Hegelian Idealism\|Hegel's Ideas]]). This approach amounts to [[note/The Primacy of Existence vs The Primacy of Consciousness\|the primacy of consciousness]] and thus, as [[people/Ayn Rand\|Ayn Rand]] puts it, to the advocacy of [[note/The Axiom of Consciousness\|consciousness]] *without* [[note/Existence Exists\|existence]].[^1]

[^1]: [[notes/OPAR\|notes/OPAR]]OPAR]], p. 30
