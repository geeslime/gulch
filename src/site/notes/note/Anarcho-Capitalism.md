Anarcho-Capitalism is fundamentally [[note/Law\|a theory about what the law is]]. Defined as [[note/Austrian Economics\|Austrianism]] applied to [[G3/boob/Anarchism\|anarchism]]. Austrian economics is all about applying [[note/Praxeology\|the science of praxeology]], which is the general science of [[note/Human Action\|Human Action]], to [[note/Economics\|economics]]. All of [[note/Economics\|economics]] is derived from the core axiom of human action. Anarcho-capitalists say that we can also apply praxeology in [[note/Law\|ethics and law]]. That's where ancaps come in; we are applying praxeology to law.

Why use the term anarchism to describe this [[note/Philosophy\|philosophy]]? Anarchism is from the ancient Greek "*anarchia*," which means without a leader, or through the Latin "*anarchia*," which means without power or authority. So in the context of law, what exactly does it mean to be a leader, one with power and authority? Surely it means that you have the authority to dictate the law; you're above the rest, and you lead the rest in matters of law. Implicit in the notion of some sort of human leader with authority over the law is [[The Failure of Legal Positivism\|legal positivism]]—the thesis that the law is [[note/The Primacy of Existence vs The Primacy of Consciousness\|whatever those in power say that it is]].

Anarchism and anarcho-capitalists are surely opposed to this thesis.[^1] It is not [[note/Arbitrary\|arbitrary]] decrees or whim which form the basis of law; it is the objective nature of [[G3/poop/Conflicts\|what conflict is]] and [[G3/Argumentation Ethics\|what is justifiable]]. We should not shy away from this term; it describes the position perfectly accurately.

Now, what makes *capitalism* a useful modifier term on the anarchism? [[note/Naive Anarchism as a Package Deal\|Proto-anarchists]] were those prior to [[Murray N. Rothbard\|Murray Rothbard]]. They had a vague notion of what it meant to *rule others* or *decree* law, but they did not understand this in the same scientific terms as we can now with the implementation of praxeology. This is what makes anarcho-capitalism distinct: it is the introduction of Austrianism and praxeology wrought by Rothbard. Given this, capitalism is quite descriptive indeed. Austrian economics places a great emphasis on [[G3/The Function of the Capitalist\|the role of the capitalist]] in [[the structure of production\|the structure of production]]. Prior to [[Marginal Utily\|marginalism]], the proto-anarchists would often see situations where a capitalist would hire employees as being exploitative. [[Marginalist Economics The Completion of Anarchism\|It is thanks to Austrians, particularly Böhm-Bawerk, that we know that this is not at all the case]]. The capitalist plays an essential role in the structure of production, and he provides a service to those he employs.

Capitalism as used in "**Anarcho-Capitalism**" should not be taken as a *prescription* of a political or economic system of "*Capitalism*" or any system of societal organization whatsoever. When referring to capitalism *qua* anarcho-capitalism, it is really talking about Austrian economics. Austrian economics is a positive, [[G2/ppp/Value-Free Science\|value-free science]]; it's not stating how things *should* be done but rather *describing* how things are. [[G2/ppp/Causal-Genetic Method of Epilology\|It outlines the effects when things are arranged in a certain way]].  

Some people might impetuously say anarcho-capitalism to mean a societal system that would adhere to [[The Non-Aggression Principle\|the non-aggression principle]] for the most part. But, [[G3/The Economist vs. Objective Legal Jurist\|what that would look like in practice is a topic for economists to discuss]].
