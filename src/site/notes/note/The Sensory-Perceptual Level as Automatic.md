The [[note/Sensation\|sensory]] [[note/The Levels of Consciousness\|level of awareness]] consists of a set of fleeting impressions that are lost the moment they are no longer impinging upon the sensory organs of the creature in question; on the [[note/Sense Perception\|perceptual]] level, these impressions are retained and integrated into specific [[note/Percept\|percepts]]. Both of these levels are automatic---it is not possible for a creature to choose to not sense a given entity, as [[note/The Metaphysical Status of Sensory Qualities\|this would violate causality]]. It is also not possible to choose to not perform the retaining and integration of qualia into percepts---you cannot choose to not see a table and instead see only the fleeting, momentary sensations that make up the table. This is because [[note/The Epistemic Primacy of Sense Perception\|the perceptual stage is given]]--*[[note/Metaphysically Given\|metaphysically given]]*--in any process of cognition you do and must start with the fact of *[[note/Entity\|entities]]* which you are performing your cognition upon, but knowledge of those entities exists on the perceptual level. If perception was [[note/Free Will\|volitional]], then one could simply choose how to integrate sensory data however they wanted and they could never begin any process of cognition. I could decide that I want tables to be chairs, and chairs to be [[Hegel\|Hegel]]---I would not be able to deduce any *facts* about [[note/Existence Exists\|reality]].
